Carl Barker

Carl is at CERN for a period of six months during his undergraduate course at Brunel University, UK. Carl will be working on the server side, possibly on client authentication. Tel: 8265. E-mail: barker@cernnext.cern.ch Tim Berners-Lee Currently in the CN division. Before joining CERN, Tim worked, among other things, on document production and word processing. He developed his first hypertext system, "Inquire", in 1980, for his own use (although he is not aware of the existence of the term HyperText). With experience in word processing, software and real-time communications, Tim decided that high-energy physics needed a networked hypertext system and CERN was the ideal place to develop hypertext ideas for wide areas. Tim started the WorldWideWeb project at CERN in 1989. He wrote the application on NeXT along with most of the communication software. Telephone: 3755, E-mail: timbl@info.cern.ch


Robert Cailliau

reviously in programming language design and compiler building, Robert has been interested in document production since 1975, when he designed and implemented a widely used document formatting and marking system. He headed CERN's Office Computing Systems group, 87-89. He is a longtime user of Hypercard, which he used for purposes as diverse as writing travel reports, games, accounting software and budget preparation forms. When he is not working on WWW public relations, Robert is contributing browser software to the Macintosh platform and analyzing the needs for physics experiments to access online data. Provided some useful information about "design problems". During his stay at CERN as a "cooperator", JF joined the project in September 1991.
