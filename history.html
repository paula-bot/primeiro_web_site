History to date

Some updated steps in the WWW project history are as follows:


March 1989

First project proposal written and circulated for comment (TBL). "HyperText and CERN" paper (in ASCII or WriteNow format) produced as a background.


October 1990

proposal reformulated with the encouragement of the administration of the CN and ECP division. RC is a co-author


November 1990

Initial prototype of WorldWideWeb developed on NeXT (TBL).


November 1990

Nicola Pellow joins and starts working on the line mode browser. Bernd Pollermann helps to get interface to the CERNVM index "FIND" in execution. TBL gives a colloquium on hypertext in general.


Christmas 1990

Demonstrable NeXTStep line mode and browsers. It is possible to access hypertext files, CERNVM "FIND" and news articles from the Internet.


February 1991

work plan for the purposes of the ECP division.


February 26, 1991

Presentation of the project to the ECP group.


March 1991

Line mode browser (www) launched for limited audience on priam vax, rs6000, sun4.


May 1991

Work plan produced for the CN / AS group


May 17, 1991

Presentation to the C5 committee. General release of www on CERN central machines.


June 12, 1991

CERN's Computer Seminar on WWW.


August 1991

Files available on the network, posted at alt.hypertext (6, 16, 19 August), comp.sys.next (20), comp.text.sgml and comp.mail.multi-media (22). Jean-Francois Groff joins the project.


October 1991

VMS / HELP and WAIS gateways installed. The www-interest (now www-Announce) and www-talk@info.cern.ch mailing lists have started. One year status report. Anonymous telnet service started.


December 1991

Poster and demonstration presented at HT91. W3 browser installed on the VM / CMS. The CERN newsletter announces W3 to the HEP world.


January 15, 1992

Line mode browser version 1.1 available via anonymous FTP. See news. Presentation for AIHEP'92 in La Londe.


February 12, 1992

V 1.2 line mode announced in alt.hypertext, comp.infosystems, comp.mail.multi-media, cern.sting, comp.archives.admin and mailing lists.


May 1992

Presentation and demonstration at JENC3 (Innsbruck). Carl Barker joins the project.


June 1992

Presentation and demonstration at HEPVM (Lyon).


July 1992

WWW distribution through CernLib, including Viola. WWW library code ported to DECnet. Reporting to the Computer Advisory Council.


August 1992

introduction of CVS for code management.
